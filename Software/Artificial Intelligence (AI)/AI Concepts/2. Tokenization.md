# 2. Tokenization

> Tokenization in AI is the foundational process of breaking down raw input data (text, images, or audio) into smaller units called tokens, which are then converted into numerical vectors (embeddings) for machine processing. It enables LLMs to understand context, predict next tokens, and manage vocabulary efficiently, acting as the primary bridge between human language and machine learning algorithms.


Tokens are tiny units of data that come from breaking down bigger chunks of information. AI models process tokens to learn the relationships between them and unlock capabilities including prediction, generation and reasoning. The faster tokens can be processed, the faster models can learn and respond.
